<p><p>The anti-reflective coatings market consists of several types based on deposition techniques. Electron beam evaporation involves using high-energy electrons to vaporize a material, creating a thin, uniform film that reduces glare and reflection. Sputtering, on the other hand, involves the ejection of material from a target onto a substrate through bombardment, resulting in a durable coating. Other methods may include chemical vapor deposition or spin coating, each offering distinct advantages in terms of efficiency and application across industries such as optics, electronics, and automotive.</p></p>
