<p><p>The Computed Tomography (CT) scanner market is categorized into three main types based on slice capacity. Low-slice scanners, with less than 64 slices, are typically used for basic diagnostics and cost-effective imaging. Medium-slice scanners, featuring 64 slices, offer improved image quality and faster scanning times, making them suitable for a wider range of clinical applications. High-slice scanners, with more than 64 slices, provide advanced imaging capabilities, allowing for detailed visualization of complex structures and faster acquisition times, beneficial for specialized diagnostics.</p></p>
