<p><p>The industrial electronic stethoscope market can be categorized into low-frequency and high-frequency segments. Low-frequency stethoscopes are designed to detect sounds in the range of 20 Hz to 500 Hz, making them ideal for monitoring large machinery and low-pitched vibrations, which can indicate potential mechanical issues. High-frequency stethoscopes, on the other hand, operate in the range of 1 kHz to 20 kHz, allowing for detailed analysis of high-pitched sounds and electrical signals, crucial for diagnosing faults in electrical systems and smaller components.</p></p>
